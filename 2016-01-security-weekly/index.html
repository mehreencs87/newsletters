Data Breach Exposes Data of 3.3 Million Hello Kitty Fans
The personal information of over three million Hello Kitty million customers, which includes children, has been found online by security researchers over the weekend. The information exposed included full names, birth dates, gender, email and password hint questions and answers. Sanrio and the ISP used to host the database were informed of the incident, which is thought to be the result of an improperly configured MongoDB database. You can read more about in CNET.
News & Upcoming Trends
Let's Encrypt: The Free Certificate Authority
Let's Encrypt is a free, automated and open certificate authority developed by the Internet Security Research Group (ISRG). It provides free X.509 certificates through an automated process designed to eliminate the current complex process of manual creation, validation, signing, installation, and renewal of certificates for secure websites. Certificates are valid for 90 days; which both limits damage from key compromise and mis-issuance.

Password Free Logins is a Reality
Passwordless Login eliminates the need of hard-to-remember passwords by using one-time codes or "magic links" delivered via SMS or email. In this way, only users who have access to the registered email account or registered phone will be able to login.
 Big companies such as Yahoo, and more recently Google, are already working on implementing this.

Will 'Internet of Things' be the Next Target of Cyber Criminals?
2015 was the year when the Internet of Things (IoT) became one of the world’s fastest growing technology sectors, and simultaneously one of the fastest growing sources of security vulnerabilities for both consumers and enterprises.
As more and more devices become tied into the Internet, the insufficient security mechanisms will likely expand the security threats in this industry. Consumers are aware of this situation, but it won’t stop them from using IoT devices.
