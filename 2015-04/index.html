Auth0 News  
  April 2015  
  Hello! Since there’s no such thing as a slow news month at Auth0, we’ll get right to it with some updates. You can also check out our Release Notes anytime.  
  Auth0 Europe Launches  
  Faster logins for everyone! We’re happy to announce that Auth0 now has two datacenters - one in the US and one in Europe. If you do business in Europe, we’ve got you covered - it’s easy to connect to the EU datacenter or migrate from
an existing US connection. Read more or contact us if you’d like to migrate.		  
   
 
 

Use Auth0 with React.js

and React Native		  
  Speaking of fast...you can now access tutorials for using Auth0 with React.js and React Native. We knew your app wouldn’t be complete without using Auth0 for authentication so go ahead and finish that project!  
   
 
 

Connect Auth0 to your

Email Service Provider		  
  Make your marketing team happy. Connecting your ESP to Auth0 means you can still take advantage of easy welcome emails and forgot password notifications straight from your dashboard but you’ll also be able to collect and view valuable data like open and click-through rates through your ESP. Connect via API or through your dashboard.  
   
 
  More of the Good Stuff  
  Deploy rules within your own source control. You can now choose to deploy rules within your own source control instead of always coming back to the Auth0 dashboard. Learn how.  
 
  Limitd is now open source. Limitd is a simple deamon for rate limiting (helping to prevent DDoS attacks) that we’ve been using internally for a while but have recently made available as an open source project.
Check it out.		  
 
  Vulnerabilities in JSON web token libraries. You may have already read the post on our blog detailing critical vulnerabilities in JSON web token libraries. Don’t worry - Auth0 customers are not affected - but if you use JSON web tokens outside of Auth0 you may want to read the full story.  
 
  As always, we’re here to help. Email support@auth0.com, chat with us at
chat.auth0.com or ask questions at ask.auth0.com, anytime.		  
  Happy Authenticating,  
  The Auth0 Team
